Overview

Designing trust through digital experiences

RIMAC's digital touchpoints had grown fragmented, inconsistent interfaces, unclear navigation, and an experience that felt transactional rather than supportive.

A refreshed brand created the opportunity to humanize insurance: one cohesive visual language, applied consistently across web and mobile.

Playground proposals for Mi Mundo RIMAC, the redesigned customer experience. Click to expand.

The Design Challenge

Three problems, one visual language

Fragmented experiences

Every product lived in its own silo, forcing customers to relearn each interface.

Difficult navigation

Finding coverage or filing a claim took too many steps, at the worst possible moments.

Inconsistent visual language

The new brand hadn't reached the product. Digital felt disconnected from RIMAC's identity.

Key Design Decisions

Design thinking, not deliverables

Making insurance feel approachable

Plain language, warm colour, and friendly iconography replaced legal-sounding labels, without losing precision.

Balancing simplicity with information density

Policies carry real complexity. Progressive disclosure keeps the surface calm while the detail stays one tap away.

Designing for trust

Clear status, honest timelines, and no dead ends, especially in claims, where anxiety is highest.

Creating consistency between web and mobile

One token set, one component library, so every touchpoint feels like the same product.
